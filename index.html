About Me

Welcome! I’m a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Engineering, driven by a passion for building intelligent systems that solve real-world problems. My work spans machine learning, computer vision, and neuroscience-inspired AI, with projects ranging from tumor tracking and tiny object detection to efficient coding models for depth perception. I’ve had the opportunity to work in both academic labs and fast-paced startup environments, including two internships at a medical imaging startup, where I collaborated across engineering, imaging, and data science teams to develop scalable pipelines and improve AI model performance. These experiences sharpened my ability to adapt quickly, communicate across functions, and deliver high-impact solutions in real-world settings.

Beyond research, I enjoy mentoring students, contributing to cross-disciplinary projects, and finding creative inspiration in photography, nature walks and gardening. I believe curiosity and collaboration are at the heart of innovation.

6D Pose Agnostic Detection of Tiny Symmetric Objects(LEGO)

Developing and implementing a comprehensive end-to-end pipeline for data collection, curation, and cleaning, alongside the creation of deep learning models to achieve robust 6D pose-agnostic detection of small symmetric objects.

Efficient Coding Of Neural Spontaneous Activity For Depth Perception

Developing a model for the LGN/V1 spontaneous activity efficient coding framework, comparing the effectiveness of efficient coding models, such as Independent Component Analysis (ICA), against traditional methods for calculating disparity maps in binocular vision. Designing physiologically appropriate filters for multimodal data, including audio, images, and video, with the aim of creating a pipeline that mimics brain behavior and learning.

Analyzing Gender Polarity In Short Text

Conducted data analysis on short social media texts using BERT to examine gender polarity, with a specific focus on the role of emojis and emoticons. (accepted in CSCI2024)
